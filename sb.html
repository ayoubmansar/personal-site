Small Business Website Transformation


Drag the slider to the left or right to see before & after of the homepage



Overview

+



Blue Monkey Tea is a tea business in Pittsburgh. Their website was built in 2009 on Yahoo's platform for small businesses and has not been changed since. Unfortunately, their website in their current form does not match the energy and passion that the ownner, Margaret Harris, puts in to her business. I set out to revitalize her website using modern web design standards, web accessibility, and user centered design. This website redesign was strictly made for a course project (67-250).

Their "Contact" page prior to redesign


Redesign

+

Blue Monkey Tea's sitemap currently has hundreds of pages. Moreover, information is often repeated across pages. In conforming to modern web design practices, I needed to significantly reduce the number of pages and reduce this repetition.

Planning
Before writing any code, I spent a lot of time drafting low and high fidelity wireframes, creating user stories organized by priority, and user testing materials & protocols. I learned a great deal about the importance of design documentation and it really helped me figure out my priorities and the ways in which I would simplify their current website.

Implementation
The website makes use of no pre-existing HTML/CSS frameworks or templates. By sticking to my design documentation, I was able to maintain a consistent design style throughout the site. The website employs a next-gen scrolling layout, common in many modern pages. This eliminates the needs for multiple pages when one page can include all of that content in a scrollable form. The homepage has been changed so that it's exciting and engaging with a video background.
